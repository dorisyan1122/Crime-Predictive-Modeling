Prediciting Crime Rate in the U.S. in 2016

  • Inspired by Rudolph, and Ahern (2018), we want to use socioeconomic status indicators and urban planning indicators to predict violent and property crime rate in the U.S. in 2016

  • Data from National Neighborhood Data Archive (NaNDA)

    • Outcome Variable: crime rate (2010 for predictive and 2016 for implementation)
    • Predictor Variables: socioeconomic status and urban planning features (street connectivity, school counts, land cover, and pollution sites)

  • Method

    1). Data wrangling 2). Predictive model building: Ridge regression, Lasso regression, Random Forest, XG Boost, and MARS 3). Use the best model to predict crime rate in 2016 and compare with the actual crime rate

Results

  • Violent Crime
    • XG Boost has the lowest RMSE (0.673).
    • Use XG boost to predict crime rate in 2016 and compare: RMSE of 0.593.
    • Top three important variables are proportion of people non-Hispanic Black, Disadvantage 1, and proportion of families with Income less than 15K.
  • Property Crime
    • Random Forest has the lowest RMSE (0.474).
    • Use XG boost to predict crime rate in 2016 and compare: RMSE of 0.459.
    • Top three important variables are Disadvantage1, Proportion of female-headed families with kids, and Disadvantage2.
